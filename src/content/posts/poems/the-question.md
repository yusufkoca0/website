title: 'The Question'
published: 2026-04-14

description: 'Poem about life and death'

My precious, my wounded deer, <br>
Why wouldst thou tremble in fear? <br>
Is't not thy blade thou behold'st, <br>
Against the grief of life thou uphold'st?

The solemn ghost of time it saith <br>
To thy forworn eyes of a wraith. <br>
Blooming violas underneath now tell a story <br>
Of sleepless nights thou claim'st to be glory.

Is't not the beauty that keepeth thee awake <br>
Or the very reason that urgeth to forsake? <br>
If 'tis the erasure thy heart desireth <br>
Why hesitate to quench thy breath?

No hunter pursueth thee through the night <br>
No hound clencheth its rabid bite <br>
Yet thou still crown'st the ache <br>
As though the wounds were thine to make.

If rest be all thy soul craveth <br>
Why still walk'st among the graves? <br>
That dreamless sleep thou seek'st <br>
Is't rest or absence thou desirest?

'Tis the first bite of the bitter tree <br>
Not to ken but to deny the decree. <br>
'Tis the sole reason why thou art so eager <br>
To shed the skin so worn and meager.

The edge that once did bestowe thee thy birth <br>
Will that loosen thy bond to earth? <br>
If peace be upon thee to take or leave <br>
Why must it be thyself, thou dost unweave?
